## 2. Segmentation (MY official definition)

MY uses **dual criteria** — lower of the two applies:

| Segment | Annual sales turnover | Full-time employees |
|---|---|---|
| **Micro** | < RM 300,000 | < 5 |
| **Small (Services)** | RM 300K – < RM 3M | 5 – < 30 |
| **Small (Manufacturing)** | RM 300K – < RM 15M | 5 – < 75 |
| **Medium (Services)** | RM 3M – RM 20M | 30 – 75 |
| **Medium (Manufacturing)** | RM 15M – RM 50M | 75 – 200 |

Source: [SME Corp Malaysia](https://smecorp.gov.my/index.php/en/policies/2020-02-11-08-01-24/sme-definition), [MITI](https://www.miti.gov.my/index.php/pages/view/793).

**For YA 2024, the definition adds an ownership criterion excluding >20% foreign-owned entities.** This matters for tax incentive eligibility but not for CatatOrder targeting.

### ⚠️ Critical comparison

MY "micro" = <RM 300K (~IDR 1 miliar). ID UMKM "mikro" = <IDR 500 juta. **MY micro threshold 2x higher by revenue cutoff.** Artinya:

- MY "micro" 761K firms sebenarnya lebih "formal" dari ID UMKM mikro
- Mayoritas CatatOrder ID user fit MY "micro" definition
- Tapi segmen paling bottom dari MY informal (street vendor, kaki lima) kemungkinan tidak captured di 761K count (mereka informal, tidak SSM-registered)

## 3. Sectoral breakdown (2024)

| Sector | Share of MSMEs | Firms |
|---|---|---|
| **Services** | **84.4%** | 917,070 |
| Construction | 8.7% | 94,152 |
| Manufacturing | 5.3% | 57,187 |
| Agriculture | 1.4% | 15,630 |
| Mining & quarrying | 0.2% | 2,347 |

Within services, dominant sub-sectors:
1. **Distributive trade** (wholesale & retail + motor vehicle repair) — largest
2. **Food & beverages services** — second largest
3. **Transportation & storage**

DOSM tidak publish F&B-only count 2024 release. **Historical proxy:** 167,490 F&B establishments (2015), +5% CAGR. **Extrapolated 2024: ~250,000-280,000 F&B micro-SMEs.** This is CatatOrder's highest-fit segment.

Source: [Human Resources Online](https://www.humanresourcesonline.net/msmes-in-malaysia-employed-nearly-half-the-workforce-in-2024-with-productivity-on-the-rise-dosm).

- Trending up

**Implication:** Strong "Ibu UMKM" segment persists in MY like ID — messaging dan positioning yang bekerja di ID (celebrate home-based female entrepreneur) transferable.

## 6. Formal vs informal registration

**No single authoritative figure**, but proxies:

- SME Corp launched **MSME Status Registration System** specifically because large share operate outside SSM
- **PMKS Digital Grant MADANI** requires ≥RM 50K turnover + SSM registered — excludes informal tier
- World Bank + academic commentary: MY informal sector smaller than ID but meaningful

**Directional estimate:** 40-50% of MY micro-enterprises likely informal/unregistered. Not a hard number — needs lawyer/tax consultation for Phase 2 accuracy.

## 7. TAM / SAM / SOM modeling

### Total Addressable Market (TAM)

**761,897 microenterprises** × **avg RM 30/month ARPU ceiling** × **12 months** = **RM 274 juta/yr theoretical TAM**

### Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM) — addressable with current product

Filter layer:
- WhatsApp Business penetration (~51% of MY SMEs) → ~**388,000 micro-SME**
- Further filter to services + F&B + retail distributive: ~80% → ~**310,000**
- Further filter to "has digital skill to use SaaS" (~40% of WA users): ~**124,000**

**SAM: ~124,000 micro-SME reachable with product.**
**SAM value:** 124K × RM 30/month × 12 = **RM 44.6 juta/yr.**

### Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) — realistic 3-year capture

Conservative 5-10% capture in 3 years:
- Low: **6,200 paying customers**
- High: **12,400 paying customers**

**SOM ARR range:** RM 2.2 – RM 4.5 juta (~IDR 7.7 – 15.8 miliar)

**Comparison:** Indonesia SOM (3-year, 5% capture dari 1.3M TAM) = **65,000-130,000 customers** = **IDR 150-390 miliar ARR**.

### Ratio ID vs MY

| Metric | ID | MY | Ratio ID/MY |
|---|---|---|---|
| TAM (paying SaaS customer count) | ~1.3-2M | ~40-60K | ~30x |
| SOM 3-year (5-10% capture) | 65K-200K | 6-12K | ~10-20x |
| SOM 3-year ARR | IDR 150-390M | IDR 8-16M | ~20-25x |

**Per-capita purchasing power adjustment:** MY GDP per capita ~2.4x ID. Adjusted "value ratio" ≈ **15-20x**.

**Blunt conclusion:** MY market size 15-20x smaller than ID by effective value. **Not negligible, but clearly secondary.**

## 8. Growth trajectory

- MY MSME CAGR: ~2.0% (2015-2024)
- MY GDP CAGR projected 2025-2028: 4-5%
- Digital economy share of GDP: 22.6% (2023) → 25.5% target (end-2025) — MDEC
- SME digital adoption target: 875,000 MSMEs by 2025

**Not a hypergrowth market.** Indonesia's UMKM count has grown slower in absolute % but from much larger base, with bigger absolute capture opportunity.

## 10. Strategic read-through

1. **Smaller market, but concentrated.** MY MSME TAM ~15-25x smaller than ID by value. 56% concentrated in 4 states = easier GTM targeting.

2. **F&B + distributive trade = primary fit.** ~250-280K F&B firms + large distributive trade segment = healthy SAM for WhatsApp ordering use case.

3. **Higher purchasing power = higher ARPU ceiling.** MY can price 2-3x ID. MYR 19-39/mo vs IDR 25-50K/mo. Tapi ARPU advantage sering di-offset oleh CAC yang lebih tinggi (detail di [06-harga-ekonomi-unit.md](./06-harga-ekonomi-unit.md)).

4. **Informality = accessible segment.** 40-50% estimated informal tidak dapat tax/SSM-gated tools (Bukku, Financio). CatatOrder's "no SSM required" approach = structural advantage.

5. **Growth slower than ID.** CAGR 2% micro = bukan hypergrowth. Expect organic acquisition slower than ID equivalent.
